## About DMC embroidery floss

DMC is the most widely used embroidery thread brand, and its color numbers have become the default language of cross stitch patterns. Several other brands — including Sullivans and CXC — use DMC-compatible numbering, printing the equivalent DMC code right on their labels. Hand-dyed brands like Weeks Dye Works (weeksdyeworks.com), Classic Colorworks, and The Gentle Art publish DMC conversion charts so stitchers can substitute when needed. Each skein is six-strand divisible cotton floss — colorfast and consistent batch-to-batch.

Quick reference: White is BLANC (or B5200 for bright white), Ecru is ECRU, Black is 310\. DMC codes loosely cluster by hue (500s tend to be greens, 800s blues) but there are plenty of exceptions.

Choosing colors for a pattern usually starts with either automatic matching (the converter matches your photo to the closest DMC threads) or manual browsing (filtering this chart by color family and comparing options). For skin tones, the 400–3860 range is the standard starting point. For greens, the 500s cluster but 3345–3348 are often better for natural foliage. When in doubt, buy candidate skeins and compare them against your fabric in the light you'll stitch under — screens lie, especially for muted tones.

## FAQ

How many DMC colors are there?

In the standard solid range, DMC currently lists 489 colors of six-strand mouliné embroidery floss — the chart most cross stitch patterns refer to. This includes the full numbered range plus BLANC, ECRU, B5200, and the 01–35 range added in 2017\. Specialty lines like Étoile and Light Effects are not included in this chart.

What's the difference between BLANC and B5200?

BLANC is a standard bright white. B5200 is an even brighter, more stark white with a slightly bluish cast. For most projects BLANC is the go-to; B5200 works well when you need maximum contrast or are stitching on off-white fabric.

Why do thread colors look different on my screen?

Every display renders color differently — screens emit light while thread reflects it, so the same hue can look quite different (see the color theory guide for why). The colors in this chart are a close reference for browsing and comparison, but for critical matching you should compare real skeins in natural light.

Are DMC specialty lines included?

No. This chart covers the 489 standard solid floss colors only. Specialty lines like Étoile (sparkle), Light Effects (metallic), Color Variations (variegated), and Coloris are separate and not included.

How do I choose the right DMC thread colors for my cross stitch pattern?

Start with a reference photo or mood board, then use a DMC color chart to find the closest matches.
